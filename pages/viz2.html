Choropleth Map

For this visualization, I used a choropleth map to convey which neighborhood has the most abandoned vehicles. In this context, a vehicle is considered abandoned if it has been left in the same place for 72 hours or more. When I first began looking at this data, I expected to see many reports of abandoned vehicles in the Down Town / Financial District neighborhoods, because I reasoned that those areas see the most traffic, and are in high demand for parking if a vehicle is abandoned. However, as you can see down below, that is not the case. The Outer Sunset has by far the most reports of abandoned vehicles, which does make sense in retrospect. That neighborhood is more suburban, with many more people owning cars. Unfortunately, this is an issue common with choropleth maps. Because the majority of people with cars are in the Outer Sunset, the data is skewed that direction. The obvious fix to this is to use a ratio of vehicles so that all neighborhoods are on the same scale. However, because census data for this year is difficult to find (especially at this granularity), my only option was to show the raw count of abandoned vehicles.

For interactivity, I provided details on demand, so if a user hovers over any neighborhood on the map, a table appears with the neighborhood name, and a count of the vehicles which were reported abandoned.
